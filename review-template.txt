> *** Relevance and Timeliness: Rate the importance and timeliness of the topic addressed in the paper within its area of research.

> *** Technical Content and Scientific Rigour: Rate the technical content of the paper (e.g.: completeness of the analysis or simulation study, thoroughness of the treatise, accuracy of the models, etc.), its soundness and scientific rigour.
Valid work but limited contribution. (3)
> *** Novelty and Originality: Rate the novelty and originality of the ideas or results presented in the paper.
Some interesting ideas and results on a subject well investigated. (3)
> *** Quality of Presentation: Rate the paper organisation, the quality of text, English writing, figures and the completeness and accuracy of references.
Readable, but revision is needed in some parts. (3)
> *** Overall recommendation: What is your overall recommendation for the paper?
Borderline. (3)
> *** Strong Aspects: Comments to the author: What are the strong aspects of the paper?
Relevant problem, as predicting weather-related disruptions can support improved reliability and planning in transportation systems.
Integration of a large-scale dataset combining railway operational records with meteorological observations across multiple years.
The hierarchical weather classification system converting raw meteorological variables into operational hazard categories is a useful domain-informed idea that may improve interpretability and feature stability.
> *** Weak Aspects: Comments to the author: What are the weak aspects of the paper?
The experimental evaluation being restricted to a single station limits conclusions about generalizability across the broader railway network.
There is also a lack of baseline comparisons against alternative machine learning models or simple heuristic predictors.
Weather hazard thresholds are not fully justified relative to established meteorological literature.
Figure 3 is difficult to follow due to its placement in the document.
> *** Recommended Changes: Recommended changes. Please indicate any changes that should be made to the paper if accepted.
Expand the evaluation to include multiple stations to better demonstrate the generalizability.
Include comparisons with other models to better assess the model's relative performance.
Improve the justification of the thresholds used for defining the weather hazard categories.
Improve the layout of Figure 3 to make it easier to follow.
